### Fixed

- **C++20 detection now gives `#if __cplusplus < N`/`<= N` the opposite
  polarity from `#if __cplusplus >= N`.** Both were previously masked
  identically — the guarded arm masked, the `#else` trusted — but a
  less-than comparison's guarded arm is the pre-C++20-safe fallback and
  should be trusted as live, while its `#else` (feature-present,
  circular) should be masked instead. A header written "if older than
  C++20, do X; else do Y" could previously force `-std=gnu++20` purely
  because Y was there, breaking an unrelated, genuinely pre-C++20 use of
  the same word in the wrongly-masked-away X arm.
